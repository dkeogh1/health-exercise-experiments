## 📊 Analysis Tier 1: Quick Wins (Haiku 4.5 Recommendation)

**Best for:** Fast insights, exploration, sanity checks

### Option A: Fitness Baseline
- Total volume by activity type (miles/hours)
- Monthly trend (are you getting more consistent?)
- Longest streak without activity
- Weekly distribution (are Mondays rest days?)
- **Output:** Simple charts showing volume over time

### Option B: HR Zone Breakdown
- What % of time in Zone 1-5? (on runs)
- Easy runs vs. tempo vs. hard intervals
- HR recovery slope (fitness indicator)
- **Output:** Bar charts, weekly zone distribution

### Option C: Time-of-Day Analysis
- Morning runner or evening? (by activity type)
- Do you run faster at certain times?
- Seasonal shift in timing?
- **Output:** Heatmap of activity type by hour/day

**Why Haiku 4.5?** Fast iterations. Get baseline stats, find what's interesting, then dig deeper. Latest lightweight model.

## 🎯 Analysis Tier 2: Patterns & Trends (Sonnet 4.6 Recommendation)

**Best for:** Deeper patterns, correlations, fitness insights

### Option D: Training Load & Recovery
- Calc weekly training stress (volume × intensity approximation)
- Map activity gaps → injury risk or planned rest?
- Compare recovery weeks (low volume) vs. peak weeks
- **Insight:** Are you polarized (hard days + easy days) or middle-ground?

### Option E: Cross-Training Synergy
- Weeks with mixed run/bike activity vs. single-sport weeks
- Does cycling improve running performance? (look for pace jumps post-bike blocks)
- Complementary fitness: bike endurance → run pace improvements?
- **Insight:** Optimal balance for your fitness trajectory

### Option F: Seasonal Performance Cycles
- Performance dips (slower pace, lower HR zones) = fatigue or intentional off-season?
- Best performance windows (when were you fastest?)
- Seasonal volume patterns (build → peak → taper → recovery)
- **Insight:** When is your natural peak? Plan accordingly.

### Option G: Biometric Trends
- Average HR on easy runs (fitness proxy)
- HR variability week-to-week (consistency)
- Max HR ceiling (does it change seasonally?)
- Resting HR estimates (recovery indicator)
- **Insight:** Are you getting fitter? When's the plateau?

**Why Sonnet 4.6?** Balances speed and depth. Good for multi-factor analysis, pattern recognition. Latest mid-tier model.

## 🔬 Analysis Tier 3: Advanced Insights (Sonnet 4.6 Recommendation — Extended Reasoning)

**Best for:** Predictive insights, anomaly detection, personalized recommendations

### Option H: Effort Distribution & Training Effectiveness
- Machine-friendly: classify each activity as Easy/Tempo/Hard based on HR zones
- Quantify adherence to 80/20 rule (80% easy, 20% hard)
- Compare polarized vs. threshold-focused trainees: which is working for you?
- **Insight:** Are you training optimally for your goals?

### Option I: Anomaly Detection & Fatigue Signals
- Unusually slow runs (could signal fatigue, illness, or bad day)
- HR drift (HR rising through the run = fatigue)
- Runs with low cadence (could be biomechanical or fatigue)
- Predict risk windows for injury based on volume spikes
- **Insight:** Early warning system for overtraining

### Option J: Predictive Modeling
- Trend forecasting: if current pace trend continues, where will you be in 12 weeks?
- "If you run 3x/week, predict monthly mileage distribution"
- Best weekly structure for your fitness trajectory
- Optimal taper strategy before a target race
- **Insight:** Science-backed race readiness calculator

### Option K: Personalized Recommendations Engine
- Analyze your actual data to generate custom training plans
- "Based on your trends, here's your ideal week structure"
- "Your fitness window: [dates]. Plan races for [specific date range]"
- "Recovery gap detected. Here's a 7-day recovery protocol tailored to you"
- **Insight:** Automated coaching based on your data

### Option L: Biometric Correlation Study
- Apple Watch HR data + Garmin power data: are they aligned?
- Effort correlation: does Garmin power predict running pace?
- Find your personal "sweet spots" (effort level where you're most consistent)
- **Insight:** Cross-device validation + personalized effort zones

**Why Sonnet 4.6?** Advanced stats, predictive modeling, multi-factor optimization. The "brain" model. Latest & most capable Claude model.

## 🧠 Analysis Tier 3.5: Reasoning & Synthesis (Kimi 2.5 Recommendation)

**Best for:** Deep reasoning, causal analysis, contradictions, edge cases, long-form synthesis

### Option M: Causal Analysis & Hypothesis Testing
- **Question:** "Why did my pace drop in February?" → Kimi traces through factors
  - Volume spike? Fatigue accumulation? Illness? Intentional cutback? Weather?
  - Cross-reference HR, cadence, gaps in data
  - Surface root causes, not just correlations
- **Insight:** Understanding *why* trends happen, not just *that* they happen

### Option N: Contradiction Detection & Resolution
- Apple Watch HR vs. perceived effort (does Strava data match your memory?)
- Garmin power data vs. Strava pace (are they aligned or misaligned?)
- Runs that "shouldn't work" (e.g., high HR but fast pace = aerobic improvement?)
- **Insight:** Spot where device data conflicts, find real patterns underneath

### Option O: Multi-Domain Synthesis & Strategy
- Integrate everything: Apple Watch HR zones + Garmin power + Strava metrics + seasonal trends + personal goals
- Generate a unified "fitness narrative" (where you've been, where you are, realistic trajectory)
- Identify hidden constraints (is volume limited by work schedule? recovery? motivation?)
- Recommend holistic training strategy, not isolated tactics
- **Insight:** Personalized long-term roadmap, not just next-week advice

### Option P: Edge Case & Black Swan Detection
- Unusual patterns that don't fit normal distributions (one run that breaks the mold)
- Are these measurement errors, breakthrough moments, or red flags?
- Example: "You ran a 5-minute mile average one day. Likely causes: mislabeling, downhill route, different GPS, or genuine breakthrough?"
- **Insight:** Separate signal from noise; find real performance jumps

### Option Q: Training Philosophy Alignment
- Compare your actual training (what you do) vs. stated philosophy (what you say you do)
- Analyze internal consistency: "You say you're polarized, but are you really?"
- Suggest micro-adjustments to better align action with intention
- **Insight:** Close the gap between goals and execution

**Why Kimi 2.5?** Extended reasoning chains. Excels at causal reasoning, hypothesis testing, and synthesizing contradictions. The "detective" model.
